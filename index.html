Portfolio Optimization

Constructed the Minimum Variance Portfolio and optimized Tendency and Desired Portfolios using a variance-covariance matrix. To assess risk, I applied Value at Risk (VaR) to quantify the maximum potential loss each portfolio could face over a specified period with a given confidence level. Additionally, I utilized Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) to simulate the price movements of the portfolios and their individual assets over a three-month horizon, based on historical volatility and drift.

Credit Risk Modeling

Machine learning models such as Logistic Regression, Regularized Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting Classifier, along with explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) techniques, were utilized to construct a comprehensive risk model. Each model's robustness was evaluated based on accuracy, classification reports,underlying transparency or explainability and model complexity. Logistic Regression emerged as the most robust model, achieving an out-of-sample prediction accuracy of 89.22%, with a weighted average f1-score of 89%, emphasizing its transparency.
